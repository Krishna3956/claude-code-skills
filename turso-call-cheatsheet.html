MINUTES 0-3

1. Let Him Lead First

He'll ask about your Turso usage. Be honest:

"I'm actually not a heavy Turso user myself — I built the challenge as part of a platform I'm working on called How Well You Know. We turn product docs into interactive challenges for SaaS companies. But I'd love to hear about the new Turso Cloud stuff!"
Let him pitch Turso Cloud for 2 min. Be genuinely interested. Ask one question. Then pivot.
MINUTES 3-5

2. The Pivot — What We Do

"So the reason I reached out — we build interactive product challenges for B2B SaaS teams. Companies use these for three things:"
  • Customer onboarding — 3-min challenge instead of 20-page docs. You see which features they don't know
  • Community engagement — drop in Discord/Slack/newsletter. People share scores
  • Developer education — especially for dev tools like Turso with complex features people don't discover
MINUTES 5-8

3. Live Demo — Screen Share the Quiz

Open howwellyouknow.com/play/turso and walk through:

  • Play 2-3 questions live
  • Skip to results page
  • Show radar chart + dimension breakdown
  • Show "Powered by" badge + share button
"Imagine you drop this in your Turso Discord or onboarding email. A new dev plays it in 3 min, and you see exactly which parts of Turso they understand and where they have gaps."
MINUTES 8-10 ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT

4. Ask Questions — STOP TALKING, START LISTENING

Pick 2-3 of these:

  • "How do you guys currently handle developer onboarding?"
  • "What does the first-week experience look like for a new Turso user?"
  • "Do you do anything interactive in your community right now?"
  • "What's your biggest challenge with getting devs to discover all of Turso's features?"
LISTEN to his answers. His words tell you exactly how to frame the value.
MINUTES 10-13

5. Connect His Answers → Your Product

Based on what he said, pick ONE:

  • If onboarding: "We could customize this for your onboarding flow. Welcome email → challenge → you see knowledge gaps"
  • If community: "Monthly Turso challenge, leaderboard in Discord, drives engagement"
  • If feature discovery: "The radar chart shows exactly which features users don't know. That's actionable data"
MINUTES 13-15

6. The Close — Free Pilot

"Here's what I'd love to do — let me customize the Turso challenge with your team's input. You test it with a small group of users and see the results. No cost for the pilot. If it works, we can talk about a plan."
Do NOT mention pricing unless HE asks. If he asks: "We're at $49/mo, but for early partners we do free pilots first."

🚫 Do NOT

  • Do NOT oversell or sound scripted — it's a conversation
  • Do NOT mention pricing unless he asks
  • Do NOT lie about traction — "We just launched. A few SaaS teams are piloting it. Turso would be one of our first design partners"
  • Do NOT go over 15 min unless HE wants to keep talking
  • Do NOT panic if he keeps pitching Turso Cloud — redirect politely
