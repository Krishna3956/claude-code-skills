# How Well You Know - Product Marketing Brief

Date: March 10, 2026
Owner: Krishna Goyal
Audience: External product marketer rewriting website copy

## 1) Company and Product Snapshot

How Well You Know is a B2B SaaS platform that turns product documentation into short interactive learning challenges.

Core product outcome:
- Users learn a product in about 3 minutes through interactive challenge formats.
- Teams get measurable understanding data (completion, scores, feature-level knowledge gaps).

Current product shape:
- Hosted challenge pages (shareable links)
- Embeddable usage patterns (email, docs, community, landing pages)
- 15-question challenge format across 6 rounds
- Shareable scorecards with dimension breakdowns
- Early-access workflow with direct founder follow-up

## 2) Problem We Solve

Target companies already spend on docs, videos, webinars, and manual onboarding calls, but users still do not deeply understand product features.

Observed pain:
- Very low docs completion in typical B2B workflows
- Repetitive onboarding burden on CS teams
- Feature adoption gaps caused by low product understanding
- No clear visibility into what users actually learned

Our wedge:
- Replace passive learning formats with interactive, measurable micro-learning.
- Keep setup light and cost accessible vs enterprise LMS tools.

## 3) ICP and Buyer Context

Primary ICP:
- B2B SaaS companies (roughly mid-market)
- Have usable public docs/help content
- Care about activation, retention, and adoption
- Need an education layer that does not require LMS-level implementation effort

Primary buyer persona:
- Head of Customer Success (top priority)

Secondary buyer personas:
- VP Customer Success
- Product Marketing / Growth teams (for lead-gen and feature education use cases)
- DevRel (especially for developer tools)

Buyer motivation:
- Reduce churn driven by poor product understanding
- Improve onboarding effectiveness without scaling headcount
- Create measurable customer education outcomes

## 4) Positioning and Differentiation

Positioning statement:
- A lightweight, embeddable customer education layer for B2B SaaS teams.

What we are not:
- Not a generic consumer quiz builder
- Not an enterprise LMS replacement for large compliance programs
- Not only an interactive demo tool
- Not only in-app onboarding tooltips

Key differentiators:
- Teaches and tests in one interactive flow
- Built from existing product docs/content
- Shareable challenge format with viral scorecards
- Clear analytics on completion and feature knowledge
- Mid-market pricing and speed to launch

## 5) Core Use Cases to Message

Use case 1: Customer onboarding
- Add challenge link in welcome/onboarding sequences.
- Help new users learn core concepts before or during first product usage.

Use case 2: Community engagement
- Post challenge in Slack/Discord/community channels.
- Drive interaction and self-driven product learning.

Use case 3: Lead generation / product education marketing
- Embed challenge on website or campaign pages.
- Convert passive visitors into educated, qualified leads.

Use case 4: Documentation enhancement
- Add “test your knowledge” moments at the end of docs sections.
- Measure whether docs are actually landing.

Use case 5: Product launch enablement
- Ship challenge alongside feature announcements.
- Increase launch comprehension and adoption speed.

## 7) Messaging Priorities for the Rewrite

Primary message hierarchy:
1. Outcome: “Your users actually learn your product.”
2. Business impact: “You see what they do not understand and can fix it.”
3. Speed and ease: “Go from docs to challenge quickly.”
4. Distribution flexibility: “Share and embed anywhere customers already are.”
5. Measurability: “Track completion, scores, and feature-level gaps.”

Tone and voice guidance:
- Product-led, clear, confident, practical
- Avoid hype-heavy AI buzzwords and vague claims
- Keep copy specific to B2B product education and retention outcomes
- Sound like a modern SaaS platform, not an agency service

Copy constraints:
- Prioritize clarity over cleverness
- Keep claims defensible and concrete
- Emphasize use-case utility over abstract brand language
