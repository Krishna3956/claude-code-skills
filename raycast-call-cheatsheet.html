Raycast Call Cheat Sheet

Monday, March 16, 2026 • 5:00 PM IST • 15 minutes • Goal: get pilot signal, not compliments

MINUTES 0-2

1. Let Her Frame The Context First

Do not launch into your deck. Let her tell you why she took the call.

"Thanks for making time, Stephanie. I built the Raycast challenge as a concrete example, but before I jump in, I'd love to hear what made this interesting enough to take a call."
This tells you whether she is curious, skeptical, or already sees a use case.
MINUTES 2-4

2. The Strategic Framing

"What we're building is not just a quiz. It's a lightweight way for software teams to make product knowledge more engaging and measurable. The question I'm trying to answer is where this actually creates the most value - onboarding, community, or feature education."
  • Not a generic LMS
  • Not a brochure microsite
  • Yes to short, shareable, measurable product learning
You are positioning the category here. Do not sound like a freelance quiz-maker.
MINUTES 4-7

3. Demo Only What Matters

Open howwellyouknow.com/play/raycast and move fast:

  • Play 2 questions max
  • Skip straight to results
  • Show the score breakdown and radar chart
  • Point out this can work as a standalone link or inside a flow
"The point is not the quiz itself. The point is whether this format can teach and reveal what users actually understand."
MINUTES 7-10 ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT

4. Force Strategic Clarity

Ask these. Not all of them. Pick the sharpest 3.

  • "Where do Raycast users usually miss depth - onboarding, discovering advanced workflows, or understanding product breadth?"
  • "If this were to matter internally, who would actually care the most about it working?"
  • "Does this feel like a useful product-learning layer, or does it feel gimmicky from your side?"
  • "Would you see more value in this as a campaign/community asset or something built into user education?"
  • "What would have to be true for Raycast to even test something like this?"
Do not rescue the idea for her. If she thinks it is weak, get the real reason.
MINUTES 10-12

5. Map Her Answers To One Pilot Angle

Pick one path only based on what she says:

  • If community: "This could run as a lightweight community challenge people share and compare."
  • If onboarding: "This could sit inside the new-user journey and make product education feel less static."
  • If feature discovery: "This becomes a way to surface which parts of Raycast users know versus what they are missing."
One angle wins. Three angles confuse.
MINUTES 12-15

6. Close On A Pilot, Not Pricing

"If this feels relevant, we can run a small pilot with Raycast and see how users respond. That would give us real signal on whether this works better for community engagement, onboarding, or feature education."
If she is warm, ask: "Who else should be involved in evaluating something like this?"
Do not mention pricing unless she asks directly. You still need use-case clarity first.

What A Win Looks Like

  • She tells you the strongest use case in Raycast terms
  • You learn who the internal owner would be
  • You get either a pilot path or an intro to the right person
  • You leave with one real objection if the answer is no

Do Not Screw This Up

  • Do not talk for 10 minutes straight
  • Do not ask "So what do you think?" like a nervous founder
  • Do not pitch this as a custom service
  • Do not broaden into random industries or training software
  • Do not leave without a next step
